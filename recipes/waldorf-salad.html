Waldorf Salad

Description

A Waldorf salad is a fruit and nut salad that features a mayonnaise dressing. It was created in 1896 by Oscar Tschirky, the maître d'hôtel at New York's Waldorf-Astoria. It doesn't traditionally contain meat, but chicken is often added to make it a complete meal.

Make the dressing, stir in the rest of the ingredients, and chill until ready to serve. That's all there is to it! Find the full, step-by-step recipe with detailed instructions below.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • 3 apples — peeled, cored, and chopped

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk mayonnaise, sugar, lemon juice, and salt together in a serving bowl.
  3. Stir in apples, celery, walnuts, and raisins. Cover and chill in the refrigerator until ready to serve.
